Becoming Interview Ready

⏱ ~10 min🏆 +50 XP📖 Module 1: Sales Mindset

📖 Lesson

Everything you've learned in this module isn't just cold calling theory — it's exactly what hiring managers test for in SDR interviews.

Interviewers aren't looking for polished sales veterans. They're looking for people who demonstrate: a growth mindset (Lesson 1), resilience (Lesson 2), willingness to practice (Lesson 3), curiosity (Lesson 4), work ethic (Lesson 5), consistency (Lesson 6), and a consultative approach (Lesson 7).

If you've completed the exercises in this module, you already have real examples to draw from — even without professional sales experience. Your practice sessions, rejection logs, and daily drills ARE the experience hiring managers want to hear about.

💼 Real-World Example

Real SDR Example: Tom had no sales experience. In his interview, he said: "I've spent the last month training myself using a structured system. I've practiced my cold call opener over 200 times, logged 50+ simulated rejections, and tracked exactly which approaches work. I can show you my rejection log and my confidence progression." The hiring manager later said Tom was the most prepared entry-level candidate she'd ever seen — not because of his experience, but because of his process.

✏️ Practical Exercise

Exercise — Build Your SDR Portfolio: Create a simple document with: 1. Your best cold call opener (written out) 2. Your top 3 objection responses 3. Your rejection log (or a summary of what you learned) 4. Your daily practice routine 5. One paragraph on why you want to be an SDR

This is your "SDR portfolio." Bring it to every interview. It proves you've done the work.

💭 If a hiring manager asked you right now: 'What have you done to prepare for this role?' — do you have a concrete answer? If not, what would make your answer undeniable?

🎯 Today's Drill

Practice this interview answer aloud until it feels natural:

"I've been training myself for this role for [X weeks]. I practice my cold call opener every day, I've logged dozens of simulated rejections to find patterns, and I've built a consultative approach where I lead with questions, not pitches. I don't have years of experience, but I have a system — and I can show you exactly how I've been improving."

Focus on: steady pace, eye contact (imagine the interviewer), and genuine conviction.

🎤 Interview Question

"Why should we hire you with no experience?"

Example answer: Because I've already started doing the job. I've been training on a structured cold calling system, practicing daily, logging my rejections, and refining my approach based on data. I don't have tenure, but I have reps, a system, and proof that I'm committed to getting better every day.

💡 This is your closer. Deliver it with conviction. Managers hire for trajectory, not history.
