⏱ ~14 min🏆 +50 XP📖 Module 2: Finding Prospects

💀 Most Prospect Lists Are Graveyards

Here's what 82% of new SDR lists look like after 30 days: dead emails, wrong titles, disconnected numbers, people who left the company 6 months ago.

That's not a list. That's a time tomb.

Every bad row in your spreadsheet doesn't just waste one call — it kills your momentum, tanks your connect rate, and trains your brain to expect nothing. After 40 dead dials in a row, even the best rep starts phoning it in.

A great list isn't a nice-to-have. It's the difference between a 2% connect rate and a 20% one.

This lesson gives you the exact channels, tools, and process to build a list that's alive on arrival.

📡 The 4 Channels — Where to Find Names

Stop Googling random companies. There are exactly 4 channels worth your time as an SDR, and each one serves a different purpose:

ChannelBest ForSpeed
1. Existing NetworkWarm intros, referrals, past colleagues who match your ICPFastest
2. LinkedInTitle/company/industry search, engagement signals, mutual connectionsFast
3. Company WebsitesIdentifying org charts, press releases, leadership pagesModerate
4. Sales Intelligence ToolsBulk contact data, intent signals, technographic filtersScalable

Most reps skip straight to channel 4 and wonder why their data is stale. Start with 1 and 2. They're free, they're warm, and they close faster.

💡 Pro-Tip: Your first 20 prospects should come from LinkedIn and your existing network — not from a database. Real names you've vetted manually always outperform bulk imports.

📐 The 5-Step List Building Process

This is the exact sequence. Don't skip steps — each one filters out noise so the next one is cleaner.

  1. Start with your ICP filters — Industry, size, geography, title. This is your search criteria.
  2. Pull 50–100 names from LinkedIn or your tool of choice. Don't go wider yet.
  3. Verify contact data — Confirm email deliverability and phone numbers. Remove bounces immediately.
  4. Add a pain/trigger column — For each name, write ONE sentence: why would this person care right now?
  5. Rank by signal strength — Sort your list so the strongest trigger signals are at the top. Call those first.
📋 Prospect List Template — Recommended Columns

Set up your spreadsheet or CRM with these columns:

  • Full Name
  • Title
  • Company
  • Industry
  • Company Size (headcount or revenue)
  • Phone (direct line preferred)
  • Email (verified)
  • LinkedIn URL
  • Pain/Trigger Note — one sentence explaining why NOW
  • Source — where you found them (LinkedIn, referral, Apollo, etc.)
  • Status — Not contacted / Attempted / Connected / Meeting booked
  • Last Contacted — date of most recent touch

The Pain/Trigger Note column is the most important one. If you can't fill it in, that name isn't a prospect yet — it's still a lead.

🎯 The BANT Qualification Framework

Once you have names, you need a quick filter to separate prospects from qualified prospects. BANT gives you four questions to run every name through:

LetterStands ForThe Question
BBudgetCan this company realistically afford our solution?
AAuthorityIs this person the decision-maker, or do they influence the decision-maker?
NNeedDoes this person's company have the specific problem we solve?
TTimingIs there a reason they'd need to solve this in the next 1–3 months?

You won't always know all four before the first call. That's fine. But you need at least 2 out of 4 to justify picking up the phone. If you can't check any boxes, that name stays in the "leads" pile.

💡 Pro-Tip: "Need" and "Timing" are the two easiest to research before a call. Budget and Authority usually surface during the conversation. Don't wait for perfect information — act on strong signals.

🚫 5 Beginner Mistakes That Kill Your List

Most reps don't need a better tool. They need to stop doing these 5 things:

❌ The Mistake✅ The Fix
Building a 500-name list on day oneStart with 50 deeply researched names. Quality compounds.
Never verifying contact dataRun emails through a verification tool. Remove bounces before you dial.
No "why now" columnAdd a trigger/pain note for every single name. No note = no call.
Calling the list top to bottomRank by signal strength. Hottest triggers first, always.
Never cleaning the listReview weekly. Remove dead contacts. Add new signals. A list is a living document.

Your list is a living asset, not a one-time download. Treat it like a garden — prune the dead, water the alive, plant new seeds every week.

✏️ Practical Exercises

🔍 Exercise 1: Data Quality Audit

Take your current prospect list (or build a test list of 20 names) and score each row:

  • +1 point — Verified email
  • +1 point — Direct phone number
  • +1 point — Pain/trigger note filled in
  • +1 point — Matches ICP profile box
  • +1 point — Contacted in the last 30 days (if not new)

Any row scoring 2 or below needs work before you call it. Target: 80% of rows at 4+.

🔍 Exercise 2: Channel Sprint

Set a 30-minute timer. Use only one channel (LinkedIn recommended) and find 15 names that match your ICP.

For each name, write:

  1. Full name + title
  2. Company + size
  3. One trigger signal or pain indicator

After 30 minutes, count how many have all 3 fields completed. That's your real list size. The ones without a trigger note? Still leads.

🔍 Exercise 3: BANT Quick-Score 10 Prospects

Pick 10 names from your list. For each, check which BANT letters you can answer before the call:

  • B — Budget: Company size/revenue suggests they can afford it? (Y/N)
  • A — Authority: Is this person a decision-maker or influencer? (Y/N)
  • N — Need: Evidence of the specific problem you solve? (Y/N)
  • T — Timing: Any trigger that suggests urgency? (Y/N)

Rank your 10 names by BANT score. Call the 4/4s first, then the 3/4s. Anything below 2/4 goes back to the research pile.

💭 How many names on your current list could you explain — in one sentence — why they'd care about your solution right now? If it's less than half, your list needs surgery, not more names.

🎤 Interview Question

"Walk me through how you'd build a prospect list from scratch in your first week."

Example answer: Day one I'd define the ICP with my manager — nail down industry, company size, and target titles. Then I'd start with my existing network and LinkedIn to find 20–30 warm, verified names. For each one I'd add a pain or trigger note so every call has a reason. I'd use Apollo's free tier to fill in phone numbers and verified emails. By end of week, I'd have 50 ranked prospects with trigger signals, not 500 unresearched names.

💡 The interviewer is testing whether you think in systems or just activity. "I'd start calling" is the wrong answer. "I'd build the filter first" is the right one.

🔑 A 50-name list with trigger signals will outperform a 500-name list without them — every single time. Build less. Research more. Call the ones who'd actually care.
