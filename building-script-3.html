Value Proposition in 10 Seconds — The Problem Proposition

⏱ ~12 min🏆 +50 XP📖 Module 3: Building Your Script

🚨 Nobody Buys Solutions. They Buy Escapes from Problems.

Here's the mistake that kills 90% of cold call pitches: leading with what you sell instead of what they suffer.

Your prospect doesn't wake up thinking "I need a cloud-based workforce management platform." They wake up thinking "I can't believe I'm still doing schedules in a spreadsheet at 11pm."

That gap — between how reps pitch and how prospects think — is where calls go to die.

People don't buy solutions to problems they understand intellectually. They buy solutions to problems they feel. Your pitch has to hit the nerve, not the brain.

💡 Pro-Tip: If your pitch includes the word "platform," "solution," or "leverage" in the first sentence, you've already lost. Those are company words. Prospects speak in problems.

🔬 The Problem Proposition — 3 Steps, 10 Seconds

We call this The Problem Proposition because it starts with the problem, not the product. Three steps. Each one earns you the next.

StepWhat It DoesTime
1. Triggering ProblemName a pain they feel right now — specific, not generic~4 sec
2. One-Sentence SolutionWhat you do about that problem — plain English, no jargon~3 sec
3. Interest CTAOne question that asks for curiosity, not commitment~3 sec

That's it. 10 seconds total. If you can't deliver your value proposition in 10 seconds, you don't know it well enough.

⚡ The Pitch Formula: PROBLEM → SOLUTION → RESULT

Inside that 10-second window, your pitch follows a locked sequence. We call it The PSR Formula:

❌ Feature-First Pitch✅ Problem-First Pitch (PSR)
"We have an AI-powered scheduling platform that integrates with your existing HR systems."Problem: "Most ops managers are still building schedules manually — it eats 8-10 hours a week."
"Our solution helps companies optimize workforce allocation."Solution: "We automate that entirely — your schedule builds itself."
"Would you like to see a demo?"Result: "Our clients typically get those 10 hours back within the first month."

The left column talks about the product. The right column talks about their life. Same product. Completely different reaction.

💡 Pro-Tip: The result should be a specific, believable number — not a vague promise. "Save time" is weak. "Get 10 hours back per week" is concrete. Specificity builds trust.

🚫 The Cardinal Sin: Getting Into the Weeds

When a prospect says "Tell me more," most reps make a fatal error: they actually tell them more.

On a cold call, your job is not to explain your product. Your job is to create enough curiosity to book a meeting. The moment you start explaining features, integrations, pricing, or technical details on a cold call, you've turned a 2-minute conversation into a demo nobody asked for.

❌ Getting Into the Weeds✅ Staying at Altitude
"So basically our platform uses machine learning to analyze historical data and then...""I'd love to walk you through exactly how it works — it's easier to show than explain. Would 15 minutes Thursday work?"
"We integrate with Salesforce, HubSpot, and 40+ other CRMs...""We plug into whatever you're already using. The best way to see if it fits is a quick 15-minute call."

Curiosity books meetings. Explanations book objections.

🎯 The CTA: Ask for Curiosity, Not Commitment

Your call-to-action is not "Would you like to buy?" It's not even "Can I schedule a demo?" Those ask for commitment from a stranger. You haven't earned that yet.

Instead, use CTAs that ask for curiosity:

🔍 CTA Examples That Book Meetings
  • "Would it be worth exploring?" — Low pressure, high conversion. The word "exploring" removes commitment.
  • "Would it make sense to see if this could help?" — Frames the meeting as a diagnostic, not a pitch.
  • "I could show you exactly how [result] works in about 15 minutes — worth a look?" — Specific time + specific result.
  • "I know I caught you cold — would it be crazy to grab 15 minutes this week just to see if there's a fit?" — Acknowledges the cold call, asks for minimal investment.

Notice: none of these say "buy," "demo," or "pitch." The word choice matters. "Explore" beats "demo" by 3x in conversion because it implies no pressure.

📋 The Complete Cold Call Script Template

Here's every piece from Lessons 1-3 assembled into one framework. This is your Master Template:

🔍 Full Script Framework — All 7 Parts

1. Opener (Context-First or Permission-Based)

"Hey [Name], I noticed [specific trigger]. Quick question on that..."

2. Introduction

"This is [Your Name] with [Company] — we help [audience] [one-line result]."

3. Reason for Call (OCQ Framework)

"The reason I'm reaching out — [observation]. [Challenge]. [Question]?"

4. Problem Proposition (PSR Formula)

"[Triggering problem]. [One-sentence solution]. [Specific result]."

5. Discovery Question

"How are you currently handling [the problem]?"

6. CTA

"Would it be worth exploring how this could work for your team? I'm thinking 15 minutes — no pressure."

7. Objection Handling (covered in Lesson 4)

Total delivery time: 45-60 seconds if the prospect engages. That's the whole call.

🔍 Example: Complete Script for a SaaS Sales Tool

Opener: "Hey Sarah, you don't know me — this is a cold call. Can I get 30 seconds?"

Intro: "This is Mike with CallFlow — we help SDR teams book more meetings without adding headcount."

Reason: "I noticed you just posted 4 SDR roles. Teams scaling that fast usually see ramp time stretch past 90 days. Is that something you're dealing with?"

Problem Prop: "Most sales leaders tell us new reps take 3-4 months before they're booking consistently. We cut that to 6 weeks with guided call frameworks. Teams using us see a 40% jump in meetings within the first quarter."

Discovery: "What does your current onboarding look like for new SDRs?"

CTA: "Would it be worth 15 minutes to see how this could accelerate your new hires? No pressure either way."

🎤 Interview Question

"Pitch me your current (or last) company's product in 10 seconds."

Example answer: "Most [audience] spend [X hours] per week on [painful task]. We eliminate that entirely — [one-sentence how]. Our clients typically see [specific result] within [timeframe]." Then stop. Don't oversell. Let the silence work.

💡 If you can deliver this cleanly in an interview, the manager knows you can deliver it on a call. Practice until you can say it without thinking — that's how it sounds natural.
