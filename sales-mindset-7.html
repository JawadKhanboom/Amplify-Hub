Think Like a Consultant

⏱ ~8 min🏆 +50 XP📖 Module 1: Sales Mindset

📖 Lesson

The worst cold callers sound like they're selling something. The best ones sound like they're helping with something.

The shift from "salesperson" to "consultant" isn't about what you say — it's about how you think. A salesperson thinks: "How do I get this person to buy?" A consultant thinks: "Does this person have a problem I can actually solve?"

This mindset changes everything: your tone becomes calmer, your questions become sharper, and your pitch becomes a natural recommendation instead of a pressure tactic. Prospects can feel the difference instantly — and they respond to it.

💼 Real-World Example

Real SDR Example: Kira sold digital marketing services. Instead of pitching features, she'd say: "I was looking at your Google Business profile and noticed a few things that might be costing you leads. Would it be helpful if I walked you through what I saw?" This wasn't a pitch — it was a genuine observation. Prospects said yes to that conversation 40% of the time, compared to 8% with her old feature-based opener.

✏️ Practical Exercise

Exercise — The Value Audit: Before your next call (or practice call), write down three specific things you genuinely believe your product/service solves for the prospect. Not features — problems. Then rewrite your opener so it references one of those problems instead of any feature. Test it on your next call.

💭 If you were the prospect, would you want to talk to you? What would make you say yes?

🎯 Today's Drill

Rewrite this pushy opener into a consultative one:

Before: "We offer the best auto detailing leads in the city."

After (say aloud): "I noticed your shop doesn't show up in a few searches where your competitors do. Would it be worth a quick look at what's happening there?"

Focus on: genuine helpfulness, specific observation, low-pressure question.

🎤 Interview Question

"How would you describe your sales style?"

Example answer: Consultative. My goal on every call is to understand whether the prospect has a problem I can genuinely help with — not to push a product. I've found that when you lead with real value and curiosity, the sale happens naturally.

💡 Every hiring manager prefers 'consultative' over 'aggressive.' This answer signals coachability and customer focus.

🔑 Stop trying to sell. Start trying to help. The pipeline follows.
