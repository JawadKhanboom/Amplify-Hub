Objection Handling · Lesson 3 of 5

Competitor Responses

⏱ ~13 min🏆 +60 XP📖 Module 6: Objection Handling

🥊 "We Already Have Someone"

An incumbent objection means a competitor already exists in this account — not that the door is closed. The prospect is telling you the current option is working well enough not to look elsewhere yet. Your job is to find out whether "well enough" is actually true.

💡 Never attack the competitor by name. Trashing a rival makes you look insecure and makes the prospect defend a choice they already made — the opposite of what you want.

🅲 Clarify Fit, Not Flaws

Instead of listing what the competitor does wrong, ask questions that let the prospect evaluate fit on their own terms:

AskWhat it reveals
"What's working well with your current setup?"Genuine strengths you shouldn't argue against
"Is there anything you wish worked differently?"A real gap, if one exists — without you naming it first
"What would make switching worth the effort?"The bar you actually need to clear
"How are you currently measuring whether it's paying off?"Whether the incumbent's value is even being tracked

If nothing is wrong and no gap exists, the honest move is to say so and offer to stay in touch — pressuring a satisfied customer of someone else usually backfires and burns the relationship for later.

🎯 Position on Fit, Not on Comparison

When a real gap does surface, respond with your fit for that specific gap — not a feature-by-feature battle against the competitor.

❌ Comparison mode✅ Fit mode
"They don't have X, Y, or Z like we do.""You mentioned reporting takes too long — that's exactly the gap we're built to close."
"Our competitor has bad reviews.""What would good look like for the thing you said isn't working?"
Reciting your full feature listSpeaking only to the one gap the prospect named

Comparison mode invites the prospect to defend their current vendor. Fit mode invites them to solve their own stated problem — with your help.

✏️ Practical Exercises

🔍 Exercise 1: Rewrite the comparison

Rewrite this response to remove the competitor comparison and focus on fit: "We already have a provider." → "Honestly, they're missing three features we've had for years — you're leaving value on the table."

🔍 Exercise 2: Ask, don't argue

Write the next three questions you'd ask after a prospect says "we're happy with our current vendor," without mentioning the competitor by name at all.

🔍 Exercise 3: The honest exit

Practice saying out loud: a prospect confirms their current setup genuinely has no gaps. Write and say the honest, low-pressure response that keeps the door open for later without pushing.

💭 The last time a prospect mentioned a competitor, did you ask what's working for them — or did you start listing what's wrong with the other option?
