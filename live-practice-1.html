The Improvement Loop

⏱ ~10 min🏆 +100 XP📖 Module 9: Live Practice

📞 What Live Call Practice Really Is

Live call practice is not "make more calls." Volume alone repeats whatever habits you have — good or bad.

Real practice is a loop: prepare, call, listen, take notes, review, and deliberately work on one specific weakness.

Most reps treat calling like cardio — more reps, more sweat, same form. But if your form is broken, running faster just gets you injured faster. The goal isn't more calls. The goal is better calls, stacked one on top of the other.

This lesson gives you the loop that makes that happen.

🔍 Know the Difference

Before jumping into the loop, get clear on the five types of practice. Each one serves a different purpose:

TypeDefinition
Script PracticeRehearsing exact wording alone or with a partner, before testing on a real prospect
RoleplayPractising a full conversation with someone playing the prospect, including pushback
Live CallingThe real thing — unscripted, unpredictable, with a real person
Call ReviewListening back or reflecting immediately after to identify what worked
CoachingA second person helping you see blind spots in your own calls
💡 All five matter. Skipping straight to live calling without review and correction just means repeating the same mistakes at higher volume.

🔄 The Live-Call Improvement Loop

This is the core framework. Six steps, repeated on every call. Each step has a clear purpose, a concrete action, and a common trap to avoid.

1 Prepare

Purpose
Walk into the call knowing who you're calling and why.
What to do
Research the prospect, write your opener, plan two discovery questions.
Avoid
Improvising with zero context.

2 Practise

Purpose
Rehearse the parts most likely to trip you up before you're live.
What to do
Say your opener and discovery questions out loud, or roleplay them with a partner.
Avoid
Practising silently in your head only.

3 Perform

Purpose
Make the actual call.
What to do
Stay present, listen more than you talk, follow the conversation.
Avoid
Reading a script word-for-word regardless of the prospect's response.

4 Review

Purpose
Learn from what actually happened.
What to do
Replay the call (where legally permitted) or write notes immediately after.
Avoid
Moving to the next call without reflecting on this one.

5 Correct

Purpose
Fix one specific weak spot.
What to do
Pick the single biggest issue and rewrite or rehearse that one moment.
Avoid
Trying to fix ten things at once.

6 Repeat

Purpose
Apply the fix on the next real call.
What to do
Use the corrected version live, then start the loop again.
Avoid
Treating the correction as a one-time fix instead of a habit to embed.

📋 Preparing Before the Call

Step 1 of the loop deserves its own deep dive. Before you dial, know:

  • Prospect's name — correct pronunciation
  • Role and company
  • A relevant trigger — news, hiring, a post, a company change
  • A possible problem tied to that trigger
  • Your specific reason for calling
  • Your opening line
  • Two discovery questions
  • How you'll offer the next step
  • The one or two objections most likely to come up

This isn't a 30-minute research project. It's a 3-minute scan that makes you sound like someone worth talking to instead of another stranger reading a list.

✅ Pre-Call Checklist

Tap each item to check it off. Use this before every call until it becomes second nature.

  • Do I know how to pronounce their name?
  • Do I know their role and one relevant fact about their company?
  • Do I have a specific, honest reason for calling today?
  • Have I written (not memorized word-for-word) my opening line?
  • Do I have two discovery questions ready?
  • Do I know what I'm offering as the next step?
💡 Coach's Note: Preparation should take minutes, not hours. If you're spending 20 minutes researching one prospect, you won't make enough calls to get good. Enough context to sound relevant — that's the bar.
