🚗 Auto Detailing

5 scripts
1. Cold Call — New Residential Prospect
Calling a homeowner who's never used a detailing service before.
📞 Opener
"Hey, is this [Name]? Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from [Your Company] — we detail cars right here in [Town/Neighborhood]. I'll keep this quick — I'm not calling to sell you anything today, just wanted to introduce ourselves since we're doing a few driveways on your street this week."
🔍 Discovery
"Out of curiosity — when's the last time your car actually got a real detail, not just a drive-through wash? ... Yeah, that's normal, most people wait until it really bothers them. What's usually the thing that annoys you most — the outside, or more the inside?"
🛡 Objection Handling
"I usually just wash it myself."
"Totally get it — a lot of my customers did too. The difference is we're pulling stuff out of the carpets and cracks a hose never touches. Most people are shocked at what comes out. Want me to show you free on the first visit, no pressure?"
"How much does it cost?"
"Depends on the size and condition — for most sedans it's between $X and $Y. Rather than quote blind, can I take two minutes to look at it in person and give you an exact number? Costs you nothing to find out."
🤝 Close
"I've actually got Thursday afternoon or Saturday morning open this week — which works better for a quick look at your car?"
2. Reactivation Call — Past Customer
Calling someone who used your service before but hasn't returned in a while.
📞 Opener
"Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] from [Your Company] — we detailed your [Car Model] back in [Month]. Hope it's still looking sharp! I was going through our records and realized we haven't seen you in a bit, so I wanted to personally check in."
🔍 Discovery
"How's the car holding up? ... Nice. Are you still parking outside, or garaged now? That usually changes how fast it needs another round."
🛡 Objection Handling
"It's been fine, I don't think it needs it yet."
"Fair enough — most cars go 3 to 4 months before it's really worth it. Want me to just pencil you in for around then so you don't have to remember to call?"
"I've been busy, maybe another time."
"No worries at all. Since you're already in our system, want me to text you a link so you can book whenever it's actually convenient — no call needed?"
🤝 Close
"As a returning customer I can get you 15% off if you book this week — want me to lock in a time before I let this slot go?"
3. B2B Call — Car Dealership Partnership
Pitching a recurring detailing contract for used-car lot prep.
📞 Opener
"Hi, this is [Your Name] with [Your Company] — is the person who handles your used-car reconditioning or lot prep available? ... Great, quick reason for the call: we detail vehicles for a couple of dealerships in the area to get them lot-ready faster, and I wanted to see if that's something you're currently outsourcing or handling in-house."
🔍 Discovery
"How are you handling detailing right now — in-house team, or a vendor? ... Got it. What's your average turnaround time from trade-in to lot-ready right now?"
🛡 Objection Handling
"We already have someone who handles this."
"Makes sense, most dealerships do. Quick question — are they able to turn a car around same-day when you're slammed, or does it back up your lot? That's usually the gap we fill as a backup vendor."
"We're not looking to switch vendors."
"Totally understand, not asking you to switch anything today. Would it be worth a free trial detail on one vehicle so you have us as an option if your current vendor ever falls behind?"
🤝 Close
"Can I swing by this week and detail one car on the house, so you can see the turnaround and quality for yourself?"
4. Inbound Follow-Up — Google/Yelp Lead
Calling someone who submitted a quote request online.
📞 Opener
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Your Company] — you requested a quote for a detail online, I'm just following up. Good time to talk for a minute?"
🔍 Discovery
"Tell me about the car — what's the make and model, and what made you look into getting it detailed right now? Anything specific bothering you, like pet hair, stains, odor?"
🛡 Objection Handling
"I'm also getting quotes from a couple other places."
"Smart, you should. Just so you're comparing apples to apples — ask the other shops if their price includes interior shampoo and paint decontamination, a lot of the cheaper quotes don't. Happy to send you exactly what's included on ours."
"Your price is higher than I expected."
"I hear you. The reason is we don't rush it — most jobs take us 2-3 hours because we're doing it right the first time. If budget's the main thing, I can put together a smaller package that covers the parts that bother you most."
🤝 Close
"I can get you on the schedule this week — does morning or afternoon work better for you?"
5. Mobile Detailing — Office / Business Park
Pitching on-site detailing to employees at a workplace parking lot.
📞 Opener
"Hi, this is [Your Name] with [Your Company] — I run a mobile detailing service, and I'm reaching out to see if [Company Name] would be open to us setting up in the parking lot one day so employees can get their car detailed while they work, no time off needed."
🔍 Discovery
"Have you ever had a vendor like this come on-site before — like a food truck day or something similar? Who would be the right person to run this idea by?"
🛡 Objection Handling
"We don't really do outside vendors on-site."
"Totally fair — a lot of places say that until they see it as an employee perk instead of a vendor pitch. Would it help if I framed it that way for whoever approves it — a free perk day, not an ad?"
"I'd have to check with someone else."
"No problem — who's that, and would it be okay if I sent you a one-page rundown you could forward to them?"
🤝 Close
"Can we pencil in a trial date — say, one Friday next month — so employees can try it out and we see how it goes?"
