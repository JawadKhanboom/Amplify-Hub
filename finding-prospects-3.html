Research Before You Call

⏱ ~12 min🏆 +50 XP📖 Module 2: Finding Prospects

⏱️ The 5-Minute Edge Nobody Uses

93% of SDRs skip pre-call research entirely. They open the dialer, see a name, and wing it.

That's not efficiency. That's laziness disguised as hustle.

Here's the math: 5 minutes of research before a call increases your meeting-book rate by roughly 2.5x. On a 60-call day, that's 5 hours of research for 2–3 extra meetings per week. Those extra meetings compound into 8–12 more meetings per month — the difference between quota and unemployment.

The reps who consistently hit quota don't have better scripts. They have better intel.

🔎 The Research Checklist — What to Look For

You don't need to write a thesis. You need 3 data points that let you say something specific in the first 15 seconds of the call. Here's where to find them:

SourceWhat to Look ForTime
LinkedIn ProfileRecent posts, job change, shared connections, endorsements90 sec
Company WebsitePress releases, "About" page, leadership changes, product launches60 sec
Google NewsFunding rounds, acquisitions, layoffs, expansion announcements60 sec
Job PostingsHiring for roles that signal growth or pain (e.g., 10 new SDR roles = need for training)30 sec
Twitter/XIndustry opinions, complaints about current tools, engagement with competitors30 sec

Total time: under 5 minutes. You're not building a dossier. You're finding one sentence that proves you did your homework.

💡 Pro-Tip: The best research finding isn't about the company — it's about the person. "I saw you posted about your team doubling this quarter" hits harder than "I see your company raised a Series B."

Personalization doesn't mean flattery. It means relevance. You're proving that this call is about their specific situation, not a random dial.

🏗️ The ACA Framework — Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask

Most reps don't know how to use their research in the actual call. They find a great insight and then bury it in paragraph three of their pitch. The ACA Framework gives you a 3-step structure to deploy your research in the first 15 seconds:

StepWhat It DoesExample
AcknowledgeReference something specific about them or their company"I saw your team just closed a Series B — congrats."
ComplimentShow genuine respect for something they've done or built"Scaling from 5 to 20 reps in a quarter is no small thing."
AskBridge to the pain with a curiosity-based question"When teams grow that fast, onboarding quality usually takes a hit — is that something you're navigating?"

The entire ACA takes under 20 seconds to deliver. It doesn't feel like a pitch — it feels like a thoughtful human starting a conversation. That's the point.

💡 Pro-Tip: The "Compliment" step is where most reps get awkward. Keep it professional and specific — compliment their work, their growth, their team. Never make it personal or over-the-top.

📡 The Signal Check — Is This Call Worth Making?

Before you dial, run through this 3-filter qualifying check. If a prospect passes all three, they go to the top of your call list:

  • Filter 1 — ICP Match: Do they fit the Profile Box? (Industry, size, title, geography)
  • Filter 2 — Pain Signal: Is there evidence they have the problem you solve? (Job postings, complaints, industry trends)
  • Filter 3 — Timing Trigger: Has something happened recently that creates urgency? (Funding, leadership change, hiring spree, competitor loss)

3/3 filters = call immediately. 2/3 = call this week. 1/3 = research more first. 0/3 = they're a lead, not a prospect. Don't waste a dial.

❌ 0–1 Filters Passed✅ 3 Filters Passed
"They're in tech. Might need what we sell.""200-person SaaS company, VP Sales just hired, posting for 10 SDRs, Series B closed last month."
You're guessingYou have a reason for every word in your opener
You'll feel like you're interruptingYou'll feel like you're providing a service

✏️ Practical Exercises

🔍 Exercise: Build Your First 20-Prospect List

This is the capstone exercise for the entire module. Set aside 2 hours and build a real, call-ready list:

  1. Define your ICP — Use the framework from Lesson 1 (Profile Box + Pain Layer)
  2. Source 25 names from LinkedIn and your existing network
  3. Verify contact data — Use Apollo free tier or another tool. Remove any bounced emails.
  4. Add a Pain/Trigger Note for each — one sentence explaining why they'd care right now
  5. Run the 3-Filter Check — Score each prospect (ICP match, pain signal, timing trigger)
  6. Cut to 20 — Remove the 5 weakest. Your list should be 20 names you'd bet money on.
  7. Rank by signal strength — Strongest triggers at the top

When you're done, you should be able to pick up the phone and call #1 on your list with a specific, personalized opener. That's the standard.

🔍 7-Day Challenge: 50 Qualified Prospects

Ready to go beyond the basics? Here's your first real challenge:

Goal: Build a list of 50 fully qualified prospects in 7 days.

  • Day 1–2: Define ICP. Source 30 names from LinkedIn + network. Verify contact data.
  • Day 3–4: Research each name. Add pain/trigger notes. Run the 3-filter check.
  • Day 5: Source 25 more names from Apollo, Crunchbase, or company websites.
  • Day 6: Research and qualify the new batch. Merge with your existing list.
  • Day 7: Cut to 50. Rank by signal strength. Write a personalized ACA opener for your top 10.

Success criteria: Every name has verified contact data, a pain/trigger note, and passes at least 2 of 3 qualifying filters. If you hit 50, you have more high-quality prospects than most reps build in their first month.

🔍 Exercise: Write 3 ACA Openers

Pick 3 prospects from your list. For each one, write a complete ACA opener:

  1. Acknowledge — Reference something specific you found in research
  2. Compliment — Show genuine respect for their work or achievement
  3. Ask — Bridge to a pain-related question

Read each opener aloud. Time it. It should take under 20 seconds. If it's longer, cut words until it fits. Prospects don't give you 30 seconds — earn the next 10 with a tight ACA.

💭 Think about the last time a salesperson called you. Did they know anything about you — or did they just read a script? Which version would have earned 30 more seconds of your time?

🎤 Interview Question

"How do you prepare for a cold call?"

Example answer: I spend about 5 minutes per prospect. I check their LinkedIn for recent posts or job changes, Google News for company announcements, and their careers page for hiring patterns. I'm looking for one specific thing I can reference in the first 15 seconds — something that proves this isn't a random call. Then I structure my opener using ACA: Acknowledge something about them, Compliment their work, and Ask a question that bridges to the pain we solve.

💡 This answer shows process and discipline. Hiring managers know that reps who research before calling are reps who prepare for everything — demos, follow-ups, negotiations. It signals coachability and work ethic.

🔑 5 minutes of research. 3 qualifying filters. One personalized opener. That's the difference between sounding like every other cold caller and sounding like someone worth talking to.
