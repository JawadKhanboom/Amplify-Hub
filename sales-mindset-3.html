Confidence Comes From Repetition

⏱ ~8 min🏆 +50 XP📖 Module 1: Sales Mindset

📖 Lesson

Confidence on a cold call doesn't come from reading scripts, watching videos, or psyching yourself up in the mirror. It comes from having done the thing enough times that it feels familiar.

This is why structured practice matters more than motivation. You don't need to feel confident before you start — you need to start before you feel confident. The feeling follows the reps.

Think about the first time you drove a car. You were hyper-aware of every input — mirrors, pedals, steering, other cars. Now you drive without thinking about it. Cold calling works the same way. The first 50 calls feel like controlled panic. After 200, you're having conversations. After 500, you sound like you've been doing this for years.

💼 Real-World Example

Real SDR Example: Jenna recorded herself practicing her cold call opener every morning for 30 days straight — just 5 minutes, speaking into her phone's voice recorder. She'd listen back, notice one thing to fix, and try again. By day 15, she stopped needing to read her script. By day 25, prospects started commenting that she sounded "really natural." She hadn't changed her script at all — she'd just repeated it enough that it became hers.

✏️ Practical Exercise

Exercise — The 5-5-5 Drill: Choose your cold call opener. Set a timer for 5 minutes. Say it out loud 5 times, recording yourself each time. Listen back and pick the ONE version that sounded most natural. Tomorrow, start from that version and do it again. Repeat for 5 days. By day 5, you will sound noticeably different than day 1.

💭 What would change in your life if you practiced one sales skill for just 5 minutes every single day for 90 days?

🎯 Today's Drill

Record yourself saying this opener, then play it back:

"Hey [Name], I know I'm catching you out of the blue — I'll be quick. I work with shops like yours that are looking to fill their schedule with higher-margin jobs. Is that something you're focused on right now?"

Listen for: pace (too fast?), filler words ("um", "like"), and whether it sounds like a conversation or a performance.

🎤 Interview Question

"How do you prepare for a day of cold calling?"

Example answer: I warm up every morning with a 5-minute drill — I practice my opener out loud, listen back, and adjust before I touch the phone. It's like an athlete stretching before a game. By the time I make my first call, I've already heard my own voice at its best.

💡 This shows discipline and process — two things hiring managers value more than raw enthusiasm.

🔑 Don't wait to feel ready. Start, repeat, and let confidence catch up.
