Personalization Hooks — Discovery, Objections, and Mistakes

⏱ ~14 min🏆 +50 XP📖 Module 3: Building Your Script

🏷️ The Label Technique: Let Them Sell Themselves

Most reps treat discovery questions like a checklist. Ask question. Get answer. Move on. That's interrogation, not conversation.

The technique that turns a cold call into a real dialogue is called The Label Technique — and it works like this: ask the prospect to label their current solution before you pitch yours.

Why? Because when someone describes their current process out loud, they hear the problems themselves. You don't need to point them out. They do the work for you.

💡 Pro-Tip: The best discovery question isn't "What's your biggest challenge?" (too broad, too cliche). It's "How are you currently handling [specific thing]?" — because the answer always reveals the gap.

🔍 Core Discovery Questions

You don't need 20 questions. You need 3-5 that go deep. Here are the ones that consistently surface real problems:

QuestionWhat It Reveals
"How are you currently handling [X]?"Their process — and its weaknesses
"What happens when [X breaks/fails]?"The downstream cost they've normalized
"If you could change one thing about [X], what would it be?"Their unspoken priority
"How long has it been this way?"Duration = severity. Longer = more pain
"What have you tried so far?"Past attempts and why they failed — gold for positioning

Notice: none of these ask "What's your budget?" or "Who's the decision-maker?" Those are qualifying questions, not discovery questions. Discovery comes first. Qualification comes in the meeting.

🛡️ Objection Handling: Agree, Understand, Ask

Objections aren't rejection. They're the prospect thinking out loud. And the worst thing you can do is argue.

The framework is called Agree, Understand, Ask — or AUA. Three steps. Never skip one.

StepWhat You SayWhat It Does
Agree"That makes total sense..."Disarms defensiveness — they expected you to push back
Understand"Most people I talk to say the same thing because..."Normalizes their concern and shows you've heard it before
Ask"Quick question though — [reframe]?"Redirects back to the problem without fighting the objection

📋 Common Objections and AUA Responses

ObjectionAUA Response
"I'm not interested.""Totally fair — most people say that before they know what it is. Quick question: are you currently dealing with [problem]? If not, I'll let you go."
"We already have something for that.""Makes sense — most companies do. Out of curiosity, how's it working? Are you getting [specific result] from it?"
"Send me an email.""Happy to. So I send something relevant — what's the biggest thing on your plate right now when it comes to [area]?"
"I don't have time right now.""Totally understand. Would it make sense to grab 15 minutes later this week? I'll keep it tight."
"How much does it cost?""It depends on the setup — but before we get into pricing, would it help to see if it even fits what you need? That way we're not wasting each other's time."

Key principle: never answer an objection with a pitch. Answer it with a question. Questions keep the conversation alive. Pitches end it.

⚡ The Disqualifying Question — Your Secret Weapon

Here's a technique most reps never learn: sometimes the fastest way to book a meeting is to give the prospect a reason to say no.

We call this The Disqualifying Question. It sounds like this:

"If not, I'll let you go — are you currently dealing with [problem]?"

This works because of a psychological principle: when you give someone an easy exit, they stop defending and start engaging. The pressure drops. The shields come down. And suddenly, instead of trying to get off the phone, they're answering honestly.

💡 Pro-Tip: "If not, I'll let you go" is the most powerful 7 words in cold calling. It communicates: I'm not desperate. I'm sorting. You can leave at any time. Paradoxically, fewer people leave.

🚫 6 Mistakes That Kill Cold Calls

Every new rep makes these. The ones who stop making them fastest are the ones who book the most meetings.

🔍 The 6 Mistakes (and How to Fix Each One)
  1. Talking too much. If you're speaking more than 40% of the call, you're pitching, not sorting. Fix: ask a question every 2-3 sentences.
  2. Leading with features. "We have AI-powered..." means nothing to a stranger. Fix: lead with the problem, always.
  3. Fighting objections. "But actually..." triggers defensiveness. Fix: Agree, Understand, Ask.
  4. No clear CTA. Rambling toward "so... yeah... would you want to..." kills momentum. Fix: plan your CTA word-for-word.
  5. Skipping research. Calling blind is a coin flip with bad odds. Fix: 60 seconds of LinkedIn/job post scanning before every call.
  6. Taking rejection personally. A stranger declining a phone call is data, not a verdict. Fix: track calls, not emotions.
🔍 Exercise: Build Your First Complete Script

Using the 7-part framework from this module, write your own complete cold call script:

  1. Opener — Choose Context-First or Permission-Based
  2. Introduction — Name, company, one-sentence what-you-do
  3. Reason for Call — OCQ (Observation, Challenge, Question)
  4. Problem Proposition — PSR (Problem, Solution, Result)
  5. Discovery Question — One question from the core list
  6. CTA — "Would it be worth exploring..."
  7. Objection Response — AUA for "I'm not interested"

Then say it out loud 5 times. Time yourself. If it's over 60 seconds before the prospect speaks, cut until it fits.

🔍 Challenge: The 3-Industry Script Sprint

Pick 3 industries you sell into (or want to). For each one:

  • Write a unique OCQ reason for call using a real observation for that industry
  • Write a Problem Proposition that names a pain specific to that vertical
  • Write an AUA response for the most common objection in that industry

Time limit: 20 minutes total. Constraint breeds creativity. Don't overthink — write, then refine.

🎤 Interview Question

"How do you handle 'I'm not interested' on a cold call?"

Example answer: First, I agree — "Totally fair, most people say that before they know why I called." Then I normalize it — "That's usually because they've gotten a lot of generic sales calls." Then I redirect with a question — "Quick question though: are you currently dealing with [problem]? If not, I'll let you go." The goal isn't to overcome the objection. It's to see if there's a real problem underneath it.

💡 This is the most common SDR interview question. Having a named framework (AUA) plus a live example immediately puts you in the top 10% of candidates.

🔑 Label their current pain. Agree, Understand, Ask through objections. Give easy exits — it's the fastest way to real engagement.
